# Beaker

Beaker is EPIC's laboratory and anatomic pathology module. It handles everything from the moment a specimen is collected to when results are reported back to the ordering clinician.

## Two Main Components

### Beaker CP (Clinical Pathology)
Handles routine lab work:
- Chemistry panels (BMP, CMP, LFTs)
- Hematology (CBC, coagulation)
- Microbiology (cultures, sensitivities)
- Urinalysis
- Blood bank / transfusion medicine

### Beaker AP (Anatomic Pathology)
Handles tissue-based diagnostics:
- Surgical pathology (biopsies, resections)
- Cytology (Pap smears, FNAs, fluid cytology)
- Autopsy
- Immunohistochemistry (IHC)
- Molecular pathology / special stains

## Key Beaker Concepts

### Accessioning
The process of receiving a specimen in the lab and assigning it a unique accession number. This links the physical specimen to the electronic order.

### Resulting
Entering the results of a test or examination. In AP, this means the pathologist's diagnosis, gross description, and microscopic findings.

### Verification
The act of a pathologist or authorized user signing off on results, making them available to the ordering provider.

### Amendments and Addenda
- **Amendment**: Corrects an error in a previously signed report
- **Addendum**: Adds additional information (e.g., special stain results that came back later)

## Who Uses Beaker?

| Role | Uses Beaker For |
|------|----------------|
| Lab Technician | Accessioning, running tests, entering results |
| Pathologist | Reviewing slides, signing out cases, synoptic reporting |
| Clinician | Viewing lab/pathology results (via Chart Review) |
| Lab Manager | Quality metrics, turnaround times, workload |
